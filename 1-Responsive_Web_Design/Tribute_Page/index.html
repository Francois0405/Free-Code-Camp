Here's a time line of Marie Curie:

  • 1867 - Marie Skłodowska (later known as Marie Curie) was born in Warsaw, Poland, which was then part of the Russian Empire.

  • 1891 - Marie Skłodowska moved to Paris to pursue higher education at the Sorbonne (University of Paris).

  • 1893 - Marie Skłodowska earned her degree in physics, ranking first in her class.

  • 1894 - Marie married Pierre Curie, a fellow scientist.

  • 1896 - Marie Curie coined the term "radioactivity" and began her research on uranium.

  • 1898 - Marie and Pierre Curie discovered the elements polonium and radium.

  • 1903 - Marie Curie became the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, sharing the Nobel Prize in Physics with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel for their work on radioactivity.

  • 1906 - Pierre Curie died in a tragic accident, and Marie took over his teaching post at the Sorbonne, becoming the first woman to teach there.

  • 1911 - Marie Curie won her second Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry, for her discovery of radium and polonium and her isolation of radium.

  • 1914 - During World War I, Marie Curie worked with her daughter Irène in mobile radiography units, providing X-rays to diagnose injuries.

  • 1929 - Marie Curie became the director of the Radium Institute in Paris.

  • 1934 - Marie Curie died of aplastic anemia, likely caused by her prolonged exposure to radiation.
